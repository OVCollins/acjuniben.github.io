"

The Nigerian press is not dying — it is being suffocated by the very institutions it was built to hold accountable.

When the government moves to regulate social media, it does not call it censorship. It calls it responsibility. When a university dismisses a student journalist, it does not call it suppression. It calls it discipline. The language changes but the intention does not. And campus journalists need to understand this dynamic before they can report it properly.

CO
Collins Victory Odabi
President, ACJ UNIBEN  ·  June 25, 2025
"

Campus media is not a training ground. It is the frontline. We must stop treating student journalists as rehearsal acts.

There is a patronising assumption embedded in Nigerian journalism education: that the work students do does not fully count until they are employed somewhere else. This assumption damages student journalism before it ever begins. When a student journalist uncovers an administrative scandal, breaks a welfare story, or holds a student union leader to account — that is real journalism. Not practice. Not preparation. The thing itself.

FA
Femi Adisa
Editor-in-Chief, The Campus Voice  ·  June 20, 2025
"

Every time a university bans a campus publication, it announces its own fragility — not its authority.

Institutions that are confident in their governance do not fear scrutiny. They expect it. They invite it. The instinct to suppress a campus newspaper, threaten a student editor, or withdraw the budget of a journalism association is the instinct of an administration that already knows what will be found if anyone looks. We should look anyway. That is the job.

RN
Ruth Nnamdi
Media Law Columnist  ·  June 15, 2025
"

AI in the newsroom is not the threat. The threat is a journalism that is too comfortable with convenience to resist it.

Artificial intelligence will do everything a lazy journalist does: summarise, paraphrase, and aggregate. What it cannot do is knock on a door, sit across from a source, read a face, follow a discrepancy through three weeks of document requests, or make the editorial judgement to publish a story that a powerful person does not want told. That is still the job. Let us protect it like one.

AO
Amara Obi
Investigations Editor, ACJ UNIBEN  ·  June 10, 2025
