Our Mission

Journalism as a Public Good

ACJ UNIBEN exists to produce rigorous, ethical, and independent journalism from within Nigeria's higher education system. We train student journalists to the professional standard, publish original investigations, features, and commentary, and advocate for press freedom across our campus and beyond.

We believe campus journalism is not a rehearsal. It is the real thing — and it matters.

Our Vision

The Standard, Not the Exception

We are building the journalism organisation that UNIBEN deserves: independent, credible, and technically equipped to produce work that stands against any professional standard in Nigeria.

Our graduates carry ACJ's editorial discipline into every newsroom they enter. That is our longest-running impact.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

Independence

ACJ UNIBEN's editorial line is set by its Editorial Board alone. No faculty administration, student body, or sponsor directs our coverage. Our editorial decisions are not negotiable.

Accuracy

We verify before we publish. Every factual claim in our work is sourced, checked, and attributed. A correction is a sign of integrity — not a failure.

Accountability

We cover those who hold power on this campus and in this country — not to antagonise them, but because that is what journalism is for. No institution is off limits.

Development

Every ACJ UNIBEN member receives structured editorial training, workshop opportunities, and mentorship from working journalists in Nigerian media.

Inclusion

Our newsroom reflects the diversity of the university. We actively recruit from all faculties, departments, and backgrounds — journalism belongs to everyone.

Ethics

We operate under a written code of ethics aligned with the Nigerian Press Council's guidelines and the international standards of the Society of Professional Journalists.

Our Story

How ACJ UNIBEN Came to Be

ACJ UNIBEN began as a small group of Mass Communication students who believed that the university campus had stories worth telling — and that students were capable of telling them properly. What started as an informal editorial collective grew into the organisation it is today: structured, published, and nationally recognised.

Founded

The Founding Convocation

A group of journalism students formally constitutes ACJ UNIBEN, adopting an editorial charter and electing its first executive body.

First Edition

The Campus Voice Goes to Print

ACJ UNIBEN publishes its first print edition — a 16-page broadsheet covering the university's budget crisis and student welfare failures.

Digital Expansion

Online Publication Launches

ACJ UNIBEN launches its digital platform, expanding reach beyond the campus gate and introducing multimedia journalism to the organisation's output.

National Recognition

First NUJ Campus Media Award

ACJ UNIBEN wins its first national award from the Nigeria Union of Journalists for excellence in campus journalism, validating the organisation's editorial standard.

Today

A Full-Service Journalism Organisation

With over 200 members, a trained editorial board, and active multimedia production, ACJ UNIBEN stands as the benchmark for campus journalism in South-South Nigeria.
